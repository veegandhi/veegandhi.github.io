"Expand the vision of your analytics"

What do you like best?

The data always have to be analyzed to obtain metrics and reports that serve to improve the activity in a company. If you want your company to grow and also improve several business areas, especially those dedicated to sales and advertising, this software can be a breakthrough that drives both your company and your business image, since you can learn to better manage data obtained.

What problems are you solving with the product? What benefits have you realized?

By being able to integrate and join several data from third-party applications within Amplitude Analytics, what we achieve in our company is that we have a multitude of data in one place. As we have all these data integrated into a single system, it is much easier to check the metrics, make reports, analyze the details ... It gives us many facilities so that we can have a more global and at the same time more detailed vision of our business , our product and also our sales.

In addition to facilitating the process, with the information we have obtained over time we have also made changes in our sales and advertising system, obtaining more profitability in our company.

"Great for communicating analytics "

What do you like best?

It's easy to customize the visualizations to show the specific piece of information that you are looking for, which makes it easy to communicate with lots of different teams. Engineering wondering about the adoption of a new version? Product wondering if membership numbers are rising? Marketing wondering the effect of their latest campaign? Easy to share and easy to understand.

It is also the one place all of our platform data can be joined up. No more digging between Apple and Play Store and GA reports and attempting to join that data in a spreadsheet.

The product is also really dynamic in its ability to create Cohorts of users based on their specific actions and properties.

What do you dislike?

When you are using this to aggregate data from several different sources or platforms, it can be difficult to ensure that all of those platforms are aligned and joined as expected in Amplitude. It seems as though their product team is working to find better ways to handle this outside of your own engineering teams efforts, but there always appear to be small errors in our data that go undetected.

Recommendations to others considering the product:

Ensure that your CSM really helps you understand the details of implementation and ensures that you have a solid plan moving in. Errors during this stage can prolong the process of getting valuable data for some time.

What problems are you solving with the product? What benefits have you realized?

We are unifying our data in Amplitude, even using it to pull in info from other third parties that provide us with valuable attribution and messaging details for our customers. It is the one source of truth that our company has. This is a huge benefit when the team has a single place to look when we answer our business questions.

"Hands down the best analytics platform on the market"

What do you like best?

Their product is incredibly fast and intuitive to use. I can easily create and analyze complex user cohorts in a myriad of different ways which, as a PM, is absolutely crucial. Second, their success managers know the product inside out, promptly respond to feedback & emails, and are all around nice people. Third, I've had the pleasure of talking with members of Amplitude's product & engineering tea, and not only are they highly intelligent, but they're also genuinely passionate about what their building.

What do you dislike?

Session analysis is weak. I can't, for example, create a cohort of users who had only 1 session or >=100 sessions. You also need to hack the integration in order to measure time on page. Finally, it's impossible to remove the most current data point on a chart which is always off because it's in the process of being calculated. I also think the product team ought to make it easier for an admin to easily pin dashboards on behalf of other members.

Recommendations to others considering the product:

It's absolutely worth it.

What problems are you solving with the product? What benefits have you realized?

Better understand which features are most closely correlated with increasing our retention. Case and point: We noticed that users who activated their accounts during the sign up flow retained far better than users who were forced to do so in order to post a blog. We revamped our sign up flow to require activation up front and saw a substantial increase in D1 retention.
